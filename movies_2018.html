365 Days at the Box Office

Americans spent nearly $12 billion at the movies in 2018. The area chart below shows the gross domestic box office sales for every day in 2018, grouped either by MPAA rating or film distributor, and the bar chart shows the top 10 films, distributors and single-day box office takes for the year.

The box office for 2018 follows a weekly cyclical pattern, with spikes appearing on weekends (Fridays, Saturdays and Sundays). Spikes can also be seen around the year-end holidays (Thanksgiving and Christmas).

PG-13 movies took in 55% of the overall box office, followed by R-rated movies (24%) and PG movies (21%).

Movie-going was roughly evenly split between quarters, with a slightly higher percent in Quarter 2, which is when traditional summer tentpole movies are released (in 2018, Avengers: Infinity War kicked off the summer movie season).
